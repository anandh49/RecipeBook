    • Classic Cappuccino

    Known for its perfect balance of strong espresso, creamy steamed milk, and airy foam, this drink is a staple of Italian coffee culture.

    Tips & Tricks

      The "Microfoam" Goal: You aren't looking for big, soapy bubbles. You want "microfoam"â€”tiny bubbles that are barely visible, creating a velvety texture.The Spoon Method: If you don't have a steam wand and are frothing milk by hand or with a whisk, use a spoon to hold back the foam while you pour the hot liquid milk first, then spoon the thick foam on top at the end.

    How to make it

    Step-by-step Instructions

      • Prepare the CupWarm your cappuccino cup (5â€“6 oz size is ideal) by rinsing it with hot water. This ensures your drink stays hot longer.
      • Brew the EspressoPull a double shot of espresso directly into your pre-warmed cup. You want a rich crema (the golden-brown foam) on top.
      • Steam the MilkPour cold milk into a metal pitcher. Submerge the steam wand just below the surface of the milk. Turn on the steam.Stretch: Lower the pitcher slightly to hear a "hissing" or "paper-tearing" sound for 3â€“5 seconds. This introduces air to create foam.Spin: Submerge the wand slightly deeper and tilt the pitcher to create a whirlpool. Heat until the pitcher is too hot to touch comfortably.
      • Polish the MilkTap the pitcher firmly on the counter to pop any large air bubbles. Swirl the milk vigorously until it looks glossy and shiny like wet paint.
      • The PourPour the steamed milk into the center of the espresso. Start high to mix the milk with the coffee, then bring the pitcher spout close to the surface to float the thick foam on top.
